The Scientific Method

The Scientific Method is a set of steps that you ask to get answers to a scientific question that you may have through observations
and experiments. All observations and experiments need to be able to be replicated and be fair. A fair test occurs when you change
only one variable and keep all of the other conditions the same. The scientific method is used by scientists to find a cause and effect
relationships in nature. Scientists design an experiment so that changes to one item can cause a reaction that is predictable. The
scientific method consists of 7 steps.

  1. Ask a question
  2. Do background research
  3. Construct a hypothesis
  4. Test your hypothesis by doing an experiment
  5. Analyze your data and draw a conclusion
  6. Check your Hypothesis
  7. Communicate your results

At any time during the process of coming to a conclusion you may have to go back a few steps when new data comes in. After you
analyze your data you need to see if your original hypothesis fits with the data. If it doesnâ€™t then you will need to create a new
hypothesis and test it out with more experiments. This refinement of your hypothesis helps narrow down what the data is saying and
what the final conclusion is. A process like the scientific method that involves such backup up and repeating is called an iterative
process. Keeping a good record of your ideas and information is essential to communicating your results.
